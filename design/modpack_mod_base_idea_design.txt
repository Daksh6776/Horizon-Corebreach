# Realistic Survival Modpack - Complete Design Document

## Modpack Overview
**Name:** Horizon Corebreach
**Concept:** Transform Minecraft into a historically-accurate survival experience with realistic geology, nutrition, civilizational progression, and ecological systems. Progress from primitive Stone Age survival through multiple eras of civilization, eventually reaching the core of reality itself.

---

## Core Mod Structure

### 1. **HorizonCore** (Foundation Mod)
**Purpose:** Central API and coordination system for all other mods

**Key Functions:**
- Event management system for cross-mod communication
- Shared configuration and balancing
- Age/era progression tracking (Stone Age → Bronze Age → Iron Age → Medieval → Industrial → Modern)
- Player statistics database (nutrition, skills, technology unlocks)
- Compatibility layer for all submods
- Central data registry for ores, materials, recipes, and technologies

## Major Component Mods

### 2. **HorizonGenesis** (World Generation)
**Realistic terrain and geological systems**

**Features:**
- **Continental Formation:**
  - Realistic tectonic plate simulation
  - Distinct continents with accurate shapes (Pangaea-like or Earth-like options)
  - Oceanic and continental crust differences
  - Mountain ranges at plate boundaries
  - Rift valleys and oceanic trenches

- **Biome Distribution:**
  - Climate zones based on latitude (equatorial, tropical, temperate, polar)
  - Rain shadows behind mountain ranges
  - Realistic biome transitions
  - Continental vs oceanic climate patterns

- **Geological Layers:**
  - Accurate rock strata (sedimentary, igneous, metamorphic)
  - Ore distribution based on geological processes:
    - Gold/Copper in volcanic regions
    - Coal in ancient swamp deposits
    - Diamonds in kimberlite pipes
    - Aluminum in bauxite deposits in tropical regions
    - Rare earths in specific geological formations
  
- **Resource Distribution:**
  - Continent A: Rich in iron and coal (industrial potential)
  - Continent B: Gold, copper, and gems (trade wealth)
  - Continent C: Oil and natural gas (late-game energy)
  - Continent D: Rare earth elements (advanced technology)
  - Oceanic regions: Salt, pearls, specific fish species

### 3. **HorizonBlocks** (Advanced Building)
**Non-cubic block shapes and realistic materials**

**Features:**
- **Block Shapes:**
  - Slopes (15°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 75°)
  - Curves and arches
  - Pillars (round, square, hexagonal)

  - Carved decorative blocks
  - Bricks in various bond patterns

- **Material Properties:**
  - Structural integrity system (some blocks need support)
  - Weight and load-bearing calculations
  - Weathering and erosion over time
  - Temperature resistance (wood burns, stone doesn't)

- **Diverse Materials:**
  - 20+ wood types (oak, pine, mahogany, teak, bamboo, etc.)
  - 15+ stone types (granite, marble, limestone, sandstone, etc.)
  - Metals with different properties (strength, corrosion resistance)
  - Composite materials (concrete, reinforced materials)

### 5. **HorizonNutrition** (Realistic Food & Health)
**Complex nutritional system**

**Features:**
- **Nutritional Components:**
  - Proteins (for muscle health and healing)
  - Carbohydrates (for energy/stamina)
  - Fats (for long-term energy storage)
  - Vitamins (A, B, C, D, E, K - various effects)
  - Minerals (Iron, Calcium, Potassium, etc.)
  - Water (hydration system)
  - Fiber (digestive health)

- **Food Categories:**
  - Meats (red meat, poultry, fish) - high protein
  - Grains (wheat, rice, corn, barley) - carbohydrates
  - Vegetables (leafy greens, roots) - vitamins & minerals
  - Fruits (varying vitamin profiles)
  - Dairy (calcium, fats, protein)
  - Nuts & seeds (healthy fats, protein)
  - Legumes (protein, fiber)

- **Health Effects:**
  - Deficiency diseases (scurvy from lack of Vitamin C, etc.)
  - Balanced diet bonuses (regeneration, strength, speed)
  - Obesity from excess calories and low activity
  - Malnutrition debuffs
  - Food poisoning from spoiled food
  - Cooking improves nutritional availability

- **Food Preservation:**
  - Fresh food spoils within days
  - Smoking meats
  - Salting
  - Drying/dehydrating
  - Pickling
  - Refrigeration (later ages)
  - Canning (industrial age)

### 6. **HorizonFauna** (Realistic Animals)
**Complex animal behavior and ecology**

**Features:**
- **Animal Categories:**
  - Herbivores: Deer, horses, cattle, sheep, goats, elephants, etc.
  - Carnivores: Wolves, bears, big cats, crocodiles, etc.
  - Omnivores: Pigs, bears, chickens, etc.
  - Birds: Eagles, parrots, chickens, ducks, etc.
  - Aquatic: Fish (salmon, tuna, etc.), dolphins, sharks, etc.
  - Insects: Bees (pollination), ants, butterflies

- **Animal Behavior:**
  - Realistic AI (hunting, grazing, territorial behavior)
  - Pack/herd dynamics
  - Predator-prey relationships
  - Breeding and offspring care
  - Migration patterns
  - Hibernation in winter
  - Diurnal/nocturnal patterns

- **Taming & Domestication:**
  - Multi-stage taming process (trust building)
  - Training for specific tasks (hunting, guarding, hauling)
  - Breeding for desired traits
  - Animals age and eventually die
  - Loyalty system (animals remember treatment)
  - Combat companions (wolves, trained big cats)
  - Mounts (horses, elephants, camels)
  - Pack animals (donkeys, llamas, oxen)

- **Death & Decomposition:**
  - Dead animals become carcasses
  - Day 1-2: Fresh (harvestable for maximum meat/hide)
  - Day 3-4: Decomposing (reduced yield, risk of disease)
  - Day 5-6: Rotten (only bones remain, attracts scavengers)
  - Day 7+: Skeleton (bones can be collected)
  - Scavengers appear (vultures, crows, hyenas)
  - Can attract predators
  - Disease risk if near water sources

### 7. **HorizonAges** (Historical Progression)
**Technology and civilization advancement**

**Stone Age (0-5000 game days):**
- Hand tools (stone axes, spears, knives)
- Fire creation
- Basic shelters
- Hunter-gatherer lifestyle
- Cave paintings
- Primitive pottery
- No metal working

**Copper/Bronze Age (5000-15000 game days):**
- Copper smelting
- Bronze tools (copper + tin)
- Basic agriculture
- Animal domestication
- Permanent settlements
- Pottery wheels
- Basic writing/record keeping
- Simple sailing boats

**Iron Age (15000-30000 game days):**
- Iron smelting (requires higher temperatures)
- Steel production
- Advanced agriculture (plows, irrigation)
- Specialized tools
- Trade networks
- Written laws/contracts
- Advanced construction
- Military equipment

**Medieval Age (30000-50000 game days):**
- Castles and fortifications
- Windmills and watermills
- Advanced metallurgy
- Guilds and specialization
- Universities and formal education
- Mechanical clocks
- Improved agriculture (crop rotation)
- Plate armor and crossbows

**Industrial Age (50000-75000 game days):**
- Steam power
- Coal and oil usage
- Factories and mass production
- Railways
- Telegraph communication
- Modern medicine (antibiotics)
- Chemical processing
- Electricity generation

**Modern Age (75000+ game days):**
- Internal combustion engines
- Electronics and computers
- Advanced materials (plastics, alloys)
- Nuclear power
- Telecommunications
- Automated manufacturing
- Medical technology
- Space exploration (endgame)

- **Common:** Iron, Copper, Tin, Coal, Salt

- **Very Rare:** Diamonds, Emeralds, Rare Earth Elements
- **Fuel:** Coal, Oil, Natural Gas, Uranium

**Ore Properties:**
- **Melting Points:** (affects which age you can use them)
  - Copper: 1085°C (Bronze Age furnaces)
  - Iron: 1538°C (requires improved furnaces)
  - Tungsten: 3422°C (industrial age only)

- **Hardness:** (affects mining tools required)
- **Density:** (affects transportation)
- **Corrosion Resistance:** (affects tool durability)
- **Conductivity:** (electrical properties for late game)

**Processing:**
- Ore → Crushed Ore → Washed Ore → Concentrate → Smelted Metal → Refined Metal → Alloy

### 11. **HorizonCraft** (Advanced Crafting)
**Realistic crafting and manufacturing**

**Features:**
- **Tool Requirements:**
  - Can't craft without appropriate tools
  - Tools have durability and sharpness
  - Specialized tools for specialized tasks
  
- **Workshop Structures:**
  - Forge (metalworking)
  - Carpentry bench (woodworking)
  - Loom (textiles)
  - Alchemy lab (chemistry, medicine)
  - Kitchen (food preparation)
  - Tannery (leather working)

- **Multi-Step Processes:**
  - Example: Sword crafting
    1. Mine iron ore
    2. Smelt in furnace → iron ingot
    3. Heat in forge
    4. Hammer on anvil → blade shape
    5. Quench in oil/water
    6. Grind edge
    7. Attach handle
    8. Polish and sharpen

### 13. **HorizonSociety** (NPCs & Civilization)
**Dynamic NPC civilizations with autonomous progression**

**Core Civilization Mechanics:**

- **Independent Age Progression:**
  - NPC civilizations advance through ages autonomously
  - Can be up to 1 full age ahead of player (e.g., player in Stone Age, NPCs in Bronze Age)
  - Progression based on: resource access, population size, research completion, trade networks
  - Different civilizations progress at different rates
  - Some may stagnate due to poor resources or disasters

- **Civilization AI Director:**
  - Each settlement has goals (expansion, defense, trade dominance, religious spread)
  - Adapts strategy based on neighbors, resources, and threats
  - Remembers past interactions with player and other settlements
  - Cultural personality types (aggressive, peaceful, merchant, isolationist, expansionist)

**NPC Learning & Adaptation:**

- **Technology Espionage:**
  - NPCs can observe player's technology if they visit settlements
  - Spies can steal research if player has poor security
  - Trade agreements can include tech sharing
  - Reverse-engineering captured equipment
  - Libraries can be raided for knowledge

- **Combat Learning:**
  - NPCs adapt tactics based on previous battles
  - If you use cavalry, they develop anti-cavalry spears
  - If you use archers, they build shields and covered approaches
  - Learn from defeats and adjust army composition
  - Veterans train new soldiers more effectively

- **Economic Learning:**
  - Adjust prices based on supply/demand
  - Learn which trade routes are profitable
  - Develop counter-strategies to player monopolies
  - Form trade coalitions against player dominance
  - Embargo player if relations are poor

- **Diplomatic Memory:**
  - Remember if player helped or hurt them
  - Grudges last for generations
  - Alliances become stronger with repeated cooperation
  - Betrayal severely damages reputation across all civilizations
  - Gift-giving and aid build trust over time

**Warfare & Raiding:**

- **Raid Motivations:**
  - Resource scarcity (drought, famine, ore depletion)
  - Territorial expansion
  - Religious/cultural conflicts
  - Revenge for past grievances
  - Opportunistic (player appears weak)
  - Bandit groups (lawless NPCs)

- **Raid Types:**
  - **Pillaging Raids:** Quick strike to steal resources and retreat
  - **Conquest:** Full military invasion to capture territory
  - **Slave Raids:** Capture population for labor
  - **Punitive Expeditions:** Destroy infrastructure as punishment
  - **Religious Crusades:** Convert or destroy "heretics"
  - **Piracy:** Naval raids on coastal settlements

- **Military Composition:**
  - Early: Militia with clubs, spears, slings
  - Bronze Age: Professional soldiers, bronze weapons, chariots
  - Iron Age: Heavy infantry, cavalry, siege weapons
  - Medieval: Knights, crossbowmen, trebuchets, castle sieges
  - Industrial: Rifles, cannons, fortified positions
  - Modern: Mechanized warfare, aircraft, naval bombardment

- **Siege Mechanics:**
  - Attackers build siege camps
  - Cut off supply lines to starve defenders
  - Siege towers, battering rams, catapults
  - Tunnel under walls (medieval+)
  - Artillery bombardment (industrial+)
  - Defenders can sally forth to attack siege equipment

- **War Consequences:**
  - Destroyed buildings need rebuilding
  - Population casualties affect labor force
  - Burned crops cause famine
  - Refugees flee to other settlements (including player's)
  - Economic depression after major wars
  - Technology can be lost if all experts die

**Settlement Types & Evolution:**

- **Hunter-Gatherer Camps (Stone Age):**
  - Temporary settlements, move seasonally
  - Small population (10-30)
  - Follow animal migrations
  - Trade furs, obsidian, flint

- **Agricultural Villages (Copper/Bronze Age):**
  - Permanent settlements near water
  - Population: 50-200
  - Granaries, animal pens, workshops
  - Vulnerable to raids (wooden palisades)

- **Town Settlements (Iron Age):**
  - Population: 200-1000
  - Stone walls, guard towers
  - Markets, temples, administrative buildings
  - Surrounding farmland worked by villagers
  - Satellite villages pay tribute

- **City-States (Medieval):**
  - Population: 1000-5000
  - Castles, universities, cathedrals
  - Multiple districts (merchant, residential, industrial, noble)
  - Standing army
  - Sophisticated diplomacy and espionage

- **Industrial Cities (Industrial Age):**
  - Population: 5000-20,000
  - Factories, railways, telegraph offices
  - Pollution and health issues
  - Worker movements and strikes
  - Police force and organized crime

- **Metropolises (Modern):**
  - Population: 20,000+
  - Skyscrapers, airports, power plants
  - Diverse population and culture
  - Advanced research facilities
  - Complex political systems

**NPC Social Systems:**

- **Governance Evolution:**
  - **Tribal:** Chief makes all decisions (Stone Age)
  - **Monarchy:** Hereditary ruler, noble class (Bronze-Medieval)
  - **Oligarchy:** Council of wealthy merchants/landowners
  - **Theocracy:** Religious leaders govern
  - **Democracy:** Elected representatives (Industrial+)
  - **Each type has different diplomacy options**

- **Social Classes:**
  - Nobles/Aristocracy (own land, provide military service)
  - Merchants (control trade, accumulate wealth)
  - Artisans/Craftsmen (create goods, form guilds)
  - Farmers/Laborers (produce food, taxed heavily)
  - Clergy (religious authority, education)
  - Slaves/Serfs (owned labor, can revolt)
  - Military (professional soldiers, veterans gain status)

- **Social Mobility:**
  - Player can gain titles through service to civilizations
  - Successful merchants can buy noble titles
  - Military heroes elevated to leadership
  - Revolutions can overthrow ruling class
  - Education increases advancement opportunities (later ages)

**NPC Professions (Expanded):**

- **Resource Extraction:**
  - Miners, lumberjacks, quarrymen, fishermen, hunters, trappers
  - Foragers, herb gatherers, salt workers, oil drillers (later)

- **Food Production:**
  - Farmers, shepherds, beekeepers, dairy farmers
  - Bakers, butchers, brewers, millers, cheesemakers

- **Crafting:**
  - Blacksmiths, armorsmiths, weaponsmiths, goldsmiths
  - Carpenters, masons, roofers, plasterers
  - Tailors, weavers, tanners, cobblers (leather workers)
  - Potters, glassblowers, chandlers (candle makers)

- **Advanced Trades:**
  - Engineers, architects, surveyors
  - Alchemists, physicians, herbalists
  - Scribes, cartographers, printers (later)
  - Bankers, accountants, lawyers (medieval+)

- **Military:**
  - Soldiers, archers, cavalry, siege engineers
  - Guards, constables, generals, admirals
  - Spies, scouts, assassins

- **Service:**
  - Merchants, innkeepers, tavern owners, cooks
  - Servants, messengers, porters, stable hands
  - Entertainers, bards, actors, prostitutes

- **Intellectual:**
  - Scholars, teachers, librarians, scientists
  - Priests, monks, theologians
  - Artists, sculptors, musicians, poets

**Dynamic Settlement Events:**

- **Positive Events:**
  - Bumper harvest (food surplus, growth)
  - Discovery of nearby ore deposits
  - Birth of genius leader (accelerated research)
  - Religious festival (tourism, trade boost)
  - Invention/innovation (tech leap)
  - Alliance with powerful neighbor
  - Successful trade expedition returns

- **Negative Events:**
  - Plague outbreak (population crash)
  - Famine (crop failure, livestock disease)
  - Fire destroys district
  - Coup/civil war (internal conflict)
  - Barbarian invasion
  - Religious schism (settlement splits)
  - Economic collapse (bank failure, trade route lost)
  - Natural disaster (earthquake, flood, volcano)

**Religion & Culture:**

- **Religious Systems:**
  - Animism (Stone Age): Nature worship, shamans
  - Polytheism (Bronze-Iron): Multiple gods, temples, priests
  - Monotheism: Single deity, organized church, missionaries
  - Philosophy/Secular: Logic, science, ethics without gods

- **Religious Influence:**
  - Temples provide morale bonuses
  - Priests can declare holy wars
  - Religious festivals boost happiness
  - Inquisitions suppress heresy (or player)
  - Missionary work spreads culture
  - Sacred sites attract pilgrims (income)

- **Cultural Development:**
  - Each civilization develops unique culture over time
  - Art styles, music, literature
  - Architectural preferences
  - Language evolution (different text in signs)
  - Holidays and traditions
  - Cultural victory possible (influence all civilizations)

**Diplomacy & Interaction:**

- **Diplomatic Options:**
  - Peace treaties, trade agreements, military alliances
  - Non-aggression pacts, research agreements
  - Royal marriages (merge dynasties)
  - Vassalization (subordinate status)
  - Tribute demands (pay or face war)
  - Joint wars against third party
  - Espionage agreements (share intel)

- **Reputation System:**
  - Warmonger: Frequent aggressor, others distrust
  - Merchant: Trade-focused, welcomed everywhere
  - Diplomat: Peace broker, mediates conflicts
  - Scholar: Shares knowledge, respected
  - Tyrant: Oppresses own people, inspires revolts
  - Liberator: Frees oppressed, gains allies

- **Player Integration:**
  - Can become citizen of NPC civilization
  - Earn ranks: Peasant → Freeman → Noble → Lord → King
  - Own land, collect taxes, command troops
  - Arrange political marriages
  - Inherit titles upon death of NPCs
  - Can be exiled or executed for crimes

**Intelligence & Communication:**

- **Information Gathering:**
  - Traders bring news from distant lands
  - Spies provide detailed intelligence reports
  - Scouting parties explore unknown regions
  - Diplomatic envoys report on foreign affairs
  - Captured prisoners interrogated for info

- **Technological Exchange:**
  - Trade can include blueprint exchanges
  - Hiring foreign experts (brain drain)
  - Reverse-engineering traded goods
  - Academic exchanges between universities
  - Corporate/industrial espionage (later ages)

**NPC Long-Term Goals:**

- **Expansionist Civs:**
  - Constantly found new settlements
  - Aggressive border expansion
  - Strong military focus
  - Will eventually conflict with player

- **Merchant Civs:**
  - Establish trade routes everywhere
  - Build commercial empires
  - Less military, more economic power
  - May buy mercenaries if threatened

- **Isolationist Civs:**
  - Minimal foreign contact
  - Strong borders, defensive posture
  - Hoards resources
  - Hostile to trespassers

- **Religious Civs:**
  - Spread their faith aggressively
  - Build grand temples
  - Crusades/jihads against non-believers
  - Can convert player's settlements

- **Scientific Civs:**
  - Focus on research and innovation
  - Often 1 age ahead technologically
  - Weak militarily but have advanced weapons
  - Willing to sell/trade technology

### 19. **HorizonMagic** (Mystical Arts & Ancient Knowledge)
**Magic system that evolves with ages**

**Early Ages (Shamanism & Rituals):**
- **Stone Age Magic:**
  - Shamanic trances for visions
  - Ritual dances affect weather (small chance)
  - Cave paintings grant minor buffs
  - Spirit communication through dreams
  - Herbal mysticism (healing poultices)
  - Bone divination (predict events)

- **Bronze Age Magic:**
  - Blood sacrifices for crop fertility
  - Curse tablets (minor debuffs on enemies)
  - Protective amulets and charms
  - Oracle readings (hints about future)
  - Sacred geometry in construction
  - Ritual bronze weapons (extra damage to "evil")

**Medieval Magic (Structured Systems):**
- **Alchemy:**
  - Transmutation experiments (lead → gold rare success)
  - Philosopher's stone quest (long-term goal)
  - Elixirs of life (healing, longevity)
  - Homunculus creation (artificial life)
  - Elemental essences (fire, water, earth, air)

- **Wizardry:**
  - Spell books must be researched and copied
  - Requires reagents, not just mana
  - Spell failure can backfire spectacularly
  - Familiar bonding (magical animal companions)
  - Enchanting weapons and armor
  - Ley lines (magical energy nodes on map)

- **Divine Magic:**
  - Prayer-based healing
  - Blessings and holy wards
  - Exorcism of curses
  - Miracle events (rare, requires high faith)
  - Holy relics with passive buffs
  - Pilgrimage to sacred sites unlocks powers

**Industrial Age (Science vs Magic):**
- Magic becomes rarer, less believed
- Scientific explanations for "magic"
- Occult societies operate in secret
- Combination of technology + magic = Magitech
- Crystal-powered engines
- Aetheric communication devices

**Modern Age (Rediscovery):**
- Ancient magic rediscovered through archaeology
- Quantum mechanics reveals magic was science
- Psionic abilities awaken in population
- Genetic engineering + magic = bio-thaumaturgy
- Dimensional research opens portals
- Space exploration finds alien magic systems

**Magic Risks:**
- Overuse causes corruption (physical mutations)
- Unstable spells can explode
- Summoning can attract unwanted entities
- Curses can backfire on caster
- NPC civilizations fear and persecute mages
- Witch hunts in religious societies

### 20. **HorizonOceans** (Oceanic Exploration)
**Deep sea mechanics and naval civilization**

**Ocean Zones:**
- **Coastal Shelf (0-200m):**
  - Rich fishing grounds
  - Kelp forests
  - Coral reefs (tropical only)
  - Coastal settlements can harvest
  - Sea monsters (sharks, giant squid)

- **Open Ocean (200-1000m):**
  - Whales, dolphins, tuna
  - Sailing routes between continents
  - Storms and hurricanes
  - Pirate encounters
  - Floating debris, shipwrecks

- **Deep Ocean (1000-4000m):**
  - Requires advanced submersibles
  - Bioluminescent creatures
  - Underwater volcanic vents
  - Rare minerals (manganese nodules)
  - Ancient sunken civilizations
  - Pressure damage without protection

- **Abyssal Zone (4000m+):**
  - Pitch black, extreme pressure
  - Unique lifeforms
  - Radioactive materials
  - Prehistoric survivors (living fossils)
  - Entrance to underground ocean caverns

**Naval Technology Progression:**
- **Stone/Bronze Age:**
  - Rafts, dugout canoes
  - Coastal fishing boats
  - Limited range, follow coast

- **Iron/Medieval:**
  - Longships (Vikings), galleys
  - Sailing ships with multiple masts
  - Navigation by stars
  - Trade vessels, warships
  - Pirates and privateers

- **Industrial:**
  - Steam-powered ships
  - Ironclad warships
  - Submarine prototypes
  - Global trade networks
  - Naval supremacy determines power

- **Modern:**
  - Nuclear submarines
  - Aircraft carriers
  - Container shipping
  - Deep sea research vessels
  - Underwater habitats

**Ocean Resources:**

**Production Mechanics:**

```java
public class FactorySystem {
    private List<ItemStack> inputBuffers;
    private List<ItemStack> outputBuffers;
    private Recipe currentRecipe;
    private int productionProgress;
    private int workersAssigned;
    private float efficiency; // 0.0-1.0
    
    public void tick() {
        if (hasRequiredInputs() && hasPower() && hasWorkers()) {
            productionProgress += workersAssigned * efficiency;
            
            if (productionProgress >= currentRecipe.getProductionTime()) {
                produceOutput();
                consumeInputs();
                productionProgress = 0;
            }
        }
    }
    
    public float getEfficiency() {
        float base = 0.5f;
        base += (workersAssigned * 0.05f); // More workers = better
        base += (maintenanceLevel * 0.2f); // Well-maintained = better
        base -= (machineAge * 0.01f);       // Old machines = worse
        return Mth.clamp(base, 0.1f, 1.0f);
    }
}
```

  - Patent issues (can't save seeds)

**Vertical Farming (Modern/Future):**

- Climate-controlled
- Year-round production

- They close borders, blame player

**Sequence:**
1. **HorizonClimate**: Extended drought hits region (years)
2. **HorizonFlora**: Crops fail, yields drop 75%

### 54. **HorizonNavigation** (Exploration & Cartography)
**From getting lost to GPS precision**

**Navigation Technology Evolution:**

- **Primitive (Stone-Bronze Age):**
  - **Landmarks:**
    - Mountains, rivers, distinctive trees
    - Memory-based navigation
    - Easy to get lost
    - Oral tradition (tell others routes)
  
  - **Sun/Stars:**
    - Daytime: Sun rises east, sets west
    - Nighttime: North Star (northern hemisphere)
    - Seasonal variation (sun angle changes)
    - Unreliable in cloudy weather

- **Ancient Navigation (Iron-Medieval):**
  - **Compass (Medieval):**
    - Magnetic needle points north
    - Reliable in all weather
    - Allow ocean voyages
    - Declination (magnetic vs true north)
  
  - **Astrolabe:**
    - Measure star angles
    - Determine latitude
    - Complex to use
    - Trained navigators needed
  
  - **Dead Reckoning:**
    - Track speed and direction
    - Estimate position
    - Errors accumulate
    - Current/wind drift issues

- **Age of Exploration (Medieval-Industrial):**
  - **Sextant:**
    - More accurate than astrolabe
    - Measure sun/star angles precisely
    - Determine latitude accurately
    - Still no good longitude method (until chronometer)
  
  - **Chronometer (Marine Clock):**
    - Precise time = longitude calculation
    - Expensive, delicate
    - Revolutionary for navigation
    - Harrison's chronometer (1700s)
  
  - **Nautical Charts:**
    - Show coastlines, depths, hazards
    - Updated with discoveries
    - Trade secrets (valuable)
    - Mercator projection

- **Modern Navigation:**
  - **Radio Navigation:**
    - LORAN (radio beacons)
    - Triangulation from signals
    - Medium accuracy
    - Weather-independent
  
  - **Inertial Navigation:**
    - Gyroscopes and accelerometers
    - Track all movements from known start
    - Drift over time
    - Submarines, missiles
  
  - **GPS (Satellite Navigation):**
    - 24+ satellites
    - Triangulation from multiple signals
    - Accuracy: ~5 meters civilian, cm military
    - Requires clear sky view
    - Works anywhere on Earth
    - Smartphones ubiquitous
  
  - **Augmented Reality Navigation:**
    - Overlay directions on camera view
    - Indoor navigation (GPS doesn't work)
    - Landmark recognition AI

**Mapping Systems:**

- **Hand-Drawn Maps (Ancient-Medieval):**

  - **Topographic Maps:**
    - Contour lines (elevation)

    - Scale bars

- **Aerial/Satellite Maps (Modern):**

    - Global coverage

### 55. **HorizonInsurance** (Risk Management Industry)
**Transferring risk from individuals to institutions**

**Insurance Types:**

- **Maritime Insurance (Medieval+):**
  - First insurance type (Lloyd's of London 1600s)
  - Ship and cargo coverage
  - Piracy, storms, shipwreck
  - Premiums based on route danger
  - Syndicates share risk
  - Fraud (scuttle ships intentionally)

- **Fire Insurance (Industrial):**
  - Buildings and contents
  - After Great Fire of London (1666)
  - Fire brigades originally insurance-company run
  - Premium based on construction (wood vs stone)
  - Arson investigation

- **Life Insurance (Industrial):**
  - Pays beneficiaries on death
  - Actuarial tables (life expectancy)
  - Premiums increase with age/health risks
  - Whole life vs term life
  - Suicide clauses
  - Murder for insurance fraud

- **Crop Insurance (Industrial-Modern):**
  - Protects farmers from disasters
  - Hail, drought, flood, pests
  - Government-subsidized (many countries)
  - Prevents farm bankruptcies
  - Moral hazard (less incentive to prevent)

- **Property Insurance (Modern):**
  - Homeowners/renters
  - Earthquake, flood, tornado coverage
  - Deductibles (pay first $X)
  - Replacement value vs actual cash value
  - Depreciation

- **Auto Insurance (Modern):**
  - Liability (damage to others)
  - Collision (damage to your vehicle)
  - Comprehensive (theft, vandalism)
  - Required by law (most places)
  - Rates based on driving record, age, location

- **Health Insurance (Modern):**
  - Already in HorizonHealthcare but expanded here
  - Hospital stays, surgeries, prescriptions
  - Preventive care
  - Pre-existing conditions (coverage denied historically)
  - Premiums, deductibles, copays
  - HMO vs PPO vs single-payer

- **Liability Insurance (Modern):**
  - Professional (malpractice for doctors, lawyers)
  - General (business operations)
  - Product liability (defective products harm)
  - Protects from lawsuits

- **Unemployment Insurance (Modern):**
  - Government program typically
  - Temporary income after job loss
  - Funded by payroll taxes
  - Prevents destitution
  - Fraud (claim while working)

**Insurance Economics:**

- **Premiums:**
  - Regular payments (monthly, annual)
  - Actuarial calculation (risk × payout)
  - Profit margin
  - Higher risk = higher premium
  
- **Risk Pools:**
  - Many people pay in
  - Few collect (disasters are rare)
  - Law of large numbers
  - Spread risk across population

- **Underwriting:**
  - Assess risk before issuing policy
  - Medical exams (life insurance)
  - Inspections (property)
  - Deny high-risk applicants
  - Risk-based pricing

- **Claims:**
  - File after loss/damage
  - Adjuster investigates
  - Approve or deny
  - Payout amount negotiated
  - Disputes and appeals

**Insurance Companies:**
- Collect premiums
- Invest funds (stocks, bonds)
- Pay claims from investment returns + premiums
- Profit if claims < (premiums + investment income)
- Bankruptcy if major disaster (Hurricane Katrina style)
- Reinsurance (insurance for insurers)

**Fraud:**
- Fake claims (staged accidents)
- Arson (burn building for payout)
- Exaggerate damage
- Murder (life insurance)
- Investigation units
- Criminal prosecution

**Regulation:**
- Government oversight (prevent insolvency)
- Mandatory coverage (auto, workers comp)
- Rate approval (prevent price gouging)
- Consumer protection
- Solvency requirements (reserves)

**Social Impact:**
- Enables risk-taking (entrepreneurship, homeownership)
- Moral hazard (less careful when insured)
- Adverse selection (sick people buy health insurance)
- Inequality (poor can't afford coverage)

### 57. **HorizonLabor** (Worker Rights & Unions)
**The struggle between capital and labor**

**Labor Conditions Through Ages:**

- **Pre-Industrial:**
  - Subsistence farming (most people)
  - Guilds control crafts
  - Apprentice → Journeyman → Master
  - Long hours but own pace
  - Family-based production

- **Early Industrial:**
  - **Factory System:**
    - 12-16 hour workdays
    - 6-7 days per week
    - Dangerous machinery (no safety)
    - Child labor (age 5-6 common)
    - Low wages, no benefits
  
  - **Conditions:**
    - Poor ventilation (lung disease)
    - Repetitive stress injuries
    - Deafening noise
    - No breaks
    - Fired if injured

- **Late Industrial (Reform Era):**
  - 10-hour workday laws
  - Minimum age restrictions (14+)
  - Basic safety regulations
  - Workers' compensation (injury coverage)
  - Still dangerous, exploitative

- **Modern Era:**
  - 8-hour day, 40-hour week standard
  - Weekends off
  - Paid vacation (varies by country)
  - Overtime pay
  - Workplace safety (OSHA-style)
  - Anti-discrimination laws
  - Family leave
  - Unions common in some sectors

**Labor Unions:**

- **Formation:**
  - Workers organize collectively
  - Illegal initially (conspiracy)
  - Violence against organizers
  - Haymarket affair, Ludlow massacre-style events
  - Eventually legalized

- **Functions:**
  - **Collective Bargaining:**
    - Negotiate wages, benefits, conditions
    - Union reps meet with management
    - Contract (lasts years)
    - Strike if no agreement
  
  - **Grievance Process:**
    - Worker complaints
    - Union representation
    - Arbitration
    - Prevent arbitrary firing
  
  - **Political Action:**
    - Lobby for labor laws
    - Endorse candidates
    - Campaign donations
    - Social Democratic parties

- **Types:**
  - **Craft Unions:** Skilled trades (electricians, plumbers)
  - **Industrial Unions:** All workers in industry (auto workers)
  - **Public Sector:** Government employees (teachers, police)

**Strikes:**

- **Process:**
  - Negotiations fail
  - Vote to authorize strike
  - Walk off job (picket lines)
  - No work = no production
  - Economic pressure on employer

- **Employer Responses:**
  - **Strikebreakers (Scabs):**
    - Hire replacement workers
    - Violence on picket lines
    - Police/military intervention
  
  - **Lockout:**
    - Employer prevents workers entering
    - Offensive rather than defensive
  
  - **Negotiation:**
    - Compromise on demands
    - End strike, return to work

- **Outcomes:**
  - Union victory (demands met)
  - Employer victory (concessions from union)
  - Stalemate (partial victory)
  - Union busted (destroyed, workers fired)

**Child Labor:**

- **Historical:**
  - Normal in agricultural societies
  - Factories exploit (small hands, cheap)
  - Textile mills, coal mines
  - Education foregone
  - Stunted development

- **Reform:**
  - Minimum age laws (14, then 16)
  - Compulsory schooling
  - Hazardous work restrictions
  - Eventually eliminated (developed countries)
  - Still exists (developing world, in-game civs)

**Workplace Safety:**

- **Hazards:**
  - Industrial accidents (machinery)
  - Toxic exposure (chemicals, asbestos)
  - Ergonomic (repetitive strain)
  - Fire (Triangle Shirtwaist)
  - Collapse (building, mine)

- **Regulations:**
  - Safety equipment (helmets, gloves, respirators)
  - Guards on machines
  - Fire exits, sprinklers
  - Inspections
  - Fines for violations
  - Criminal charges (gross negligence)

- **Workers' Compensation:**
  - Employer-funded insurance
  - Cover medical costs
  - Lost wages (disability)
  - No-fault (worker doesn't prove negligence)
  - Prevents lawsuits (trade-off)

**Wage & Hour Laws:**

- **Minimum Wage:**
  - Legal floor on hourly pay
  - Prevents exploitation
  - Adjusted periodically (inflation)
  - Debate: job losses vs living wage

- **Overtime:**
  - Time-and-a-half (1.5x pay) over 40 hours
  - Incentivizes hiring more workers
  - Exempt employees (salaried professionals)

- **Maximum Hours:**
  - Prevent exhaustion
  - 8-hour day fought for decades
  - Some countries: 35-hour week

**Unemployment:**

- **Causes:**
  - Layoffs (company downsize)
  - Automation (robots replace)
  - Economic recession
  - Seasonal (construction in winter)
  - Structural (industry decline)

- **Unemployment Insurance:**
  - See HorizonInsurance
  - Temporary income support
  - Job search requirement
  - Prevents desperation (crime, starvation)

**Labor vs Capital:**

- **Class Conflict:**
  - Workers want higher wages
  - Employers want higher profits
  - Zero-sum tension
  - Strikes, lockouts
  - Political struggle (pro-labor vs pro-business parties)

- **Compromise:**
  - Social democracy (European model)
  - Strong unions + welfare state
  - Shared prosperity
  - Vs: Deregulation, union-busting (US model)

**Modern Issues:**

- **Gig Economy:**
  - Uber, DoorDash contractors
  - No benefits, protections
  - Flexibility vs precarity
  - Misclassification debates

- **Automation:**
  - Robots, AI replace workers
  - Productivity gains
  - Job losses
  - Retraining, UBI debates

- **Globalization:**
  - Jobs move to low-wage countries
  - "Race to the bottom"
  - Manufacturing hollowed out
  - Nationalist backlash

**In-Game Mechanics:**

- **Player as Employer:**
  - Hire workers for factories, farms
  - Set wages, conditions
  - Face strikes if exploitative
  - High wages = high costs but loyal workers

- **NPC Civilizations:**
  - Labor movements emerge (industrial age)
  - Unions form, strike
  - Government response varies (repress vs accommodate)
  - Socialist revolutions possible (extreme exploitation)

### 59. **HorizonWater** (Hydrology & Water Management)
**Every civilization is hydraulic**

**Water Sources:**

- **Surface Water:**
  - Rivers, lakes, streams
  - Easy access
  - Pollution vulnerable
  - Seasonal variation (droughts, floods)

- **Groundwater:**
  - Aquifers (underground water)
  - Wells (dig to reach)
  - Pumps (mechanical, electric)
  - Artesian wells (natural pressure)
  - Depletion (pumping faster than recharge)
  - Ogallala Aquifer example (US Great Plains)

- **Rainwater:**
  - Catchment systems (roofs, cisterns)
  - Seasonal (wet/dry)
  - Small-scale
  - Supplemental only

- **Desalination (Modern):**
  - Remove salt from seawater
  - Reverse osmosis or distillation
  - Energy-intensive (expensive)
  - Brine disposal (environmental issue)
  - Gulf states, Israel use extensively

**Water Infrastructure:**

- **Aqueducts (Ancient-Medieval):**
  - Roman engineering feat
  - Gravity-fed (slight downhill slope)
  - Stone construction
  - Arcades (iconic arches)
  - Bring water from distant mountains
  - Still functional (some)

- **Water Treatment (Modern):**
  - **Filtration:**
    - Sand filters (remove particles)
    - Activated carbon (chemicals)
    - UV light (kill bacteria)
  
  - **Chlorination:**
    - Disinfect (kill pathogens)
    - Residual protection in pipes
    - Slight taste/smell
    - Prevents cholera, typhoid
  
  - **Distribution:**
    - Network of pipes
    - Water towers (gravity pressure)
    - Pumping stations
    - Fire hydrants

- **Sewage Treatment:**
  - Already in HorizonUrbanization but expanded
  - **Primary:** Solids settle out
  - **Secondary:** Bacteria digest organics
  - **Tertiary:** Chemicals, nutrients removed
  - Discharge to river or reuse

**Irrigation (Agriculture):**
- See HorizonAgriculture
- Canals, ditches
- Sprinklers, drip systems
- Consume 70% of global freshwater
- Salinization (salt buildup in soil)

**Dams & Reservoirs:**

- **Purposes:**
  - **Water Storage:**
    - Reserve for dry season
    - Drinking water supply
    - Irrigation
  
  - **Flood Control:**
    - Hold back floodwaters
    - Release slowly downstream
    - Prevents devastation
  
  - **Hydroelectric Power:**
    - See HorizonPower
    - Clean, renewable
    - Major electricity source
  
  - **Recreation:**
    - Boating, fishing
    - Tourism revenue

- **Impacts:**
  - **Positive:**
    - Reliable water
    - Power generation
    - Flood prevention
  
  - **Negative:**
    - Flood upstream valley (villages displaced)
    - Sediment trapping (delta erosion)
    - Fish migration blocked (salmon)
    - Earthquake risk (reservoir-induced seismicity)
    - Methane release (decomposing plants in reservoir)
    - Three Gorges Dam example (China)

**Water Scarcity:**

- **Causes:**
  - **Physical:**
    - Arid climate (deserts)
    - Drought (climate change)
    - Aquifer depletion
  
  - **Economic:**
    - Infrastructure lacking (can't access water)
    - Pollution (water undrinkable)
    - Population growth (more demand)

- **Consequences:**
  - Crop failures (famine)
  - Health crises (dirty water, cholera)
  - Migration (climate refugees)
  - Conflict (water wars)

**Water Rights & Conflicts:**

- **Riparian Rights:**
  - Landowners along river have rights
  - Reasonable use (can't deplete for others)
  - Common law tradition

- **Prior Appropriation:**
  - "First in time, first in right"
  - Western US system
  - Senior rights (early users) vs junior

- **International Disputes:**
  - Rivers cross borders (Nile, Tigris, Euphrates, Mekong)
  - Upstream dams harm downstream countries
  - Egypt vs Ethiopia (Grand Renaissance Dam)
  - India vs Pakistan (Indus Waters Treaty)
  - Israel vs Palestine (water access)
  - Potential wars

**Water Pollution:**

- See HorizonEnvironment
- Industrial discharge
- Agricultural runoff
- Sewage
- Oil spills
- Plastic contamination

**Conservation & Efficiency:**

- **Reduce Use:**
  - Low-flow toilets, showers
  - Efficient irrigation (drip)
  - Drought-tolerant landscaping (xeriscaping)
  - Fix leaks
  - Behavioral changes (shorter showers)

- **Reuse:**
  - Greywater (sinks, showers → irrigation)
  - Reclaimed wastewater (treated sewage → irrigation, industry)
  - Toilet to tap (purify wastewater for drinking)

- **Rainwater Harvesting:**
  - Catch roof runoff
  - Store in tanks
  - Reduce demand on municipal supply

**In-Game Mechanics:**

- **Settlements Require Water:**
  - Near river/lake or dig wells
  - Aqueducts for distant sources
  - Water shortage → population decline
  - Drought events

- **Player Needs Water:**
  - Hydration mechanic (already in HorizonNutrition)
  - Find clean water
  - Purification (boil, filter)
  - Contaminated water → disease

- **Infrastructure Projects:**
  - Build dams (expensive, long-term benefit)
  - Aqueducts (engineering feat)
  - Wells (simple, local)
  - Treatment plants (modern, costly)

### 60. **HorizonWaste** (Sanitation & Disposal)
**Managing what we discard**

**Waste Types:**

- **Organic Waste:**
  - Food scraps, yard waste
  - Biodegradable
  - Composting (return nutrients to soil)
  - Methane in landfills (greenhouse gas)

- **Recyclable Materials:**
  - Paper, cardboard
  - Glass
  - Metals (aluminum, steel)
  - Plastics (#1-7, varying recyclability)
  - Contamination issues (dirty = trash)

- **Non-Recyclable:**
  - Mixed materials (chip bags)
  - Certain plastics (#3, #6 often)
  - Styrofoam
  - To landfill or incinerator

- **Hazardous Waste:**
  - Chemicals (cleaners, solvents)
  - Batteries (heavy metals)
  - Electronics (e-waste: lead, mercury)
  - Paint, pesticides
  - Medical waste (needles, infectious)
  - Radioactive (nuclear plant waste)
  - Special disposal required

**Waste Management Through Ages:**

- **Primitive:**
  - Minimal waste (organic decays)
  - Bones, shells discard piles (middens)
  - Move when area polluted

- **Ancient-Medieval:**
  - Trash in streets (animal/human waste too)
  - Disease spreads (plague, cholera)
  - Open sewers
  - Dunghills
  - Throw waste out windows (Paris)
  - Pigs roam streets eating garbage

- **Industrial:**
  - **Open Dumps:**
    - Pile trash anywhere
    - Fires, rats, disease
    - Groundwater contamination
  
  - **Incineration:**
    - Burn waste
    - Reduces volume
    - Air pollution (dioxins, particulates)
    - Ash disposal still needed

- **Modern:**
  - **Sanitary Landfills:**
    - Engineered (liners prevent leaching)
    - Compaction (more space)
    - Daily cover (reduce smell, pests)
    - Leachate collection (contaminated liquid)
    - Methane capture (energy)
    - Eventually full (space limited)
  
  - **Recycling:**
    - 1970s environmental movement
    - Separate collection (bins)
    - Processing facilities (sort, clean, reprocess)
    - Markets for materials (fluctuating prices)
    - Contamination issues (wishful recycling)
  
  - **Composting:**
    - Municipal programs
    - Food waste → compost
    - Methane reduction
    - Soil amendment
  
  - **Waste-to-Energy:**
    - Modern incinerators
    - Pollution controls (scrubbers)
    - Generate electricity
    - Expensive to build
    - Europe uses extensively

**Collection Systems:**

- **Municipal Collection:**
  - Trucks on schedule (weekly)
  - Curbside pickup
  - Driver + loaders or automated (robot arm)
  - Transfer stations (consolidate for long haul)

- **Privatization:**
  - Waste Management Inc. style companies
  - Contract with cities
  - Profit motive (efficiency or corner-cutting?)

**Recycling Economics:**

- **Profitable Materials:**
  - Aluminum (high value, infinite recyclability)
  - Cardboard (paper mills demand)
  - Steel (scrap value)

- **Unprofitable:**
  - Glass (heavy, low value, breaks)
  - Plastics (except #1, #2)
  - Contaminated items (toss all)

- **China's National Sword:**
  - 2018: China stopped accepting recycling
  - US recycling collapsed (nowhere to send)
  - Landfills or burning
  - Domestic processing insufficient

**Litter & Pollution:**

- **Ocean Plastic:**
  - Rivers carry to sea
  - Great Pacific Garbage Patch
  - Microplastics (everywhere, food chain)
  - Marine life ingests/entangles
  - Bioaccumulation (toxins concentrate)

- **Litter:**
  - Roadsides, parks, streets
  - Aesthetic blight
  - Drain clogs (flooding)
  - Cleanup costs
  - Anti-litter campaigns (fines, education)

**Illegal Dumping:**

- **Fly-Tipping:**
  - Dump in unauthorized areas (woods, roadsides)
  - Avoid disposal fees
  - Difficult to catch
  - Environmental damage
  - Cleanup costs to taxpayers

- **International Waste Trade:**
  - Rich countries export to poor countries
  - E-waste to Africa, Asia
  - Toxic recycling (burn wires for copper)
  - Health disasters (Agbogbloshie, Ghana)
  - Basel Convention (restrict hazardous waste trade)

**Circular Economy:**

- **Concept:**
  - Eliminate waste (design for reuse/recycle)
  - Products as services (lease, return)
  - Cradle-to-cradle (vs cradle-to-grave)
  - Industrial symbiosis (one's waste = another's input)

- **Examples:**
  - Bottles: Deposit-return (incentive to return)
  - Clothing: Take-back programs (recycle fibers)
  - Electronics: Right-to-repair (extend life)

**Nuclear Waste (Special Case):**

- **Types:**
  - Low-level (tools, clothing from plants)
  - Intermediate (resins, chemical sludges)
  - High-level (spent fuel rods)

- **Storage:**
  - **Short-term:**
    - Cooling pools (water shields radiation)
    - Dry cask storage (concrete/steel containers)
    - On-site at nuclear plants
  
  - **Long-term:**
    - Geological repositories (deep underground)
    - Yucca Mountain (US, never opened)
    - Finland's Onkalo (operational)
    - Stable for 10,000+ years needed
    - Political opposition (NIMBY)

- **Risks:**
  - Leakage (contaminate groundwater)
  - Terrorism (dirty bomb)
  - Accidents (transport)

**In-Game Mechanics:**

- **Settlements Generate Waste:**
  - Larger population = more waste
  - Landfill space limited
  - Pollution if improperly managed
  - Disease outbreaks (medieval dumps)

- **Player Waste:**
  - Trash items (degraded tools, spoiled food)
  - Dispose or litters world
  - Composting (return nutrients)
  - Recycling (recover materials)

- **Advanced Civilizations:**
  - Recycling programs (modern)
  - Waste-to-energy plants
  - Circular economy (best)
  - Pollution reduction

### 65. **HorizonFishing** (Commercial Fishing)

**Fishing Methods:**

- **Hook & Line:**
  - Sustainable (selective)
  - Labor-intensive
  - Artisanal, small-scale

- **Nets:**
  - **Gill Nets:**
    - Fish caught by gills
    - Bycatch issues (dolphins, turtles)
    - Drift nets (miles long, "walls of death")
    - International bans (some types)
  
  - **Trawling:**
    - Drag net along bottom
    - Scoop up everything
    - Destroy seafloor habitat
    - Massive bycatch (discard dead)
    - Industrial-scale

  - **Purse Seine:**
    - Surround school of fish
    - Tuna, sardines
    - Efficient
    - Dolphin-safe (exclude mammals)

- **Traps:**
  - Lobster, crab pots
  - Selective
  - Ghost fishing (lost traps keep catching)

**Overfishing:**

- Fish populations collapse (Atlantic cod, bluefin tuna)
- Exceed reproduction rate
- Bycatch mortality
- Trophic cascade (ecosystem imbalance)
- Economic disaster (fishing communities collapse)

**Regulations:**

- Quotas (limit catch)
- Size limits (let young reproduce)
- Seasonal closures (spawning periods)
- Marine protected areas (no fishing zones)
- Enforcement (coast guard patrols)
- Illegal fishing (poachers)

**Aquaculture (Fish Farming):**

- Raise fish in pens/tanks
- Salmon, tilapia, shrimp
- Relieves wild fish pressure
- Issues:
  - Disease spreads
  - Antibiotics, chemicals
  - Escapees (interbreed with wild, weaken genetics)
  - Feed (require wild fish, net loss sometimes)
  - Pollution (waste)

**Processing:**

- Gutting, filleting
- Freezing (preserve)
- Canning (tuna, sardines)
- Smoking, drying (traditional)
